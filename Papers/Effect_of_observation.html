Summary

1.Title of the paper The effects of observation errors on the attack vulnerability of complex networks
2. Noise Model - How did they perturb the network (did they provide an algorithm). Two attack strategies are considered: a random attack where nodes are selected for removal randomly; and, a targeted attack where nodes are selected for removal deterministically based on centrality scores.
3. What data sets did they use (also the number of edges and vertices) Collection of randomly generated synthetic graphs which are assumed to be complete. A topology erdos-renyi (Erdos & Renyi, 1959), scale-free (Barabási & Albert, 1999), small world (Watts & Strogatz, 1998), or scale-free communities (Lancichinetti & Fortunato, 2009)), a size (25, 50 or 100 nodes) and a density (0.01, 0.02, 0.05, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5).
4. What property/measures of network are they looking at A list of nodes and centrality measure (degree, betweenness, closeness, or eigenvector)
5. How do they measure the difference in results The effect of the attack on the true network is assessed by measuring the relative size of the largest connected component (Motter & Lai, 2002)
6. Are there theoretical results--write the theorems No
7. What are the main conclusions--find it in the abstract and the conclusion Proposal:
The list of targeted nodes is deleted from the true network where the effect of the attack is measured.
Conclusion:
Node deletion errors can have a significant impact even in small scale attacks. It is the source of error that has the most disruptive impact on the effectiveness of network attacks.

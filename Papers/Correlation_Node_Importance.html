1.Title of the paper Correlation of Node Importance Measures: An Empirical Study through Graph Robustness
2. Noise Model - How did they perturb the network (did they provide an algorithm) Compare and contrast 15 node-based graph attack strategies, with varying time complexities -The similarity between two strategies to be the weighted correlation between their rankings. (Rank-C) -Compare the strategies based on the characteristics of the top nodes that they target by the matching overlap and the separability between their feature vectors. (Topk-C) -Use the ranking provided by a strategy to remove the nodes one by one from a given graph. 3 clusters of highly correlated node-based strategies, namely {PageRank15, PageRank50, Betweenness}, {Katz, Eigen-vector}, and {Degree, Closeness}.
3. What data sets did they use (also the number of edges and vertices) 68 real-world graphs, spread across 4 categories (14 social, 12 biological, 36 infrastructures, and 6 information)
4. What property/measures of network are they looking at Eigen-vector or closeness centrality, degree and radius, PageRank, betweenness or closeness centrality, Katz index
5. How do they measure the difference in results -Compared the nodes picked by each attack strategy directly (Weight-Tau) or by mapping them to a feature space (Bi-Match, SVM-Sep) -Remove nodes from each graph in order of the ranked list produced by each attack strategy and this attack-driven study as another way to validate our clustering results.
6. Are there theoretical results--write the theorems No
7. What are the main conclusions--find it in the abstract and the conclusion The ﬁrst large-scale correlation analysis of attack strategies for graph robustness. Understanding of the strategies help in approximation of opportunities, where computationally expensive measures can be approximated by comparable cheaper ones, and a few strategies can be used as a proxy to an overall consensus.

Summary

1.Title of the paper Weighted Graphs and Disconnected Components
2. Noise Model - How did they perturb the network (did they provide an algorithm). Generative “Butterﬂy” model
3. What data sets did they use (also the number of edges and vertices) Both uni and bipartite, and both weighted and unweighted graphs. -- NIPS1, Arxiv and Patent patent citation graphs, no weighting scheme -- IMDB indicates movie-actor information -- Netﬂix, NetTraﬃc, BlogNet and PostNet, Auth-Conf, Key-Conf, and Auth-Key -- CampOrg and CampIndiv are bipartite graphs from U.S. Federal Election Commission -- Largest graph (CampIndiv) had 6 million nodes and 10 million edges, with annual time-stamps
4. What property/measures of network are they looking at Weighted edges, nodes, shrinking diameters, densiﬁcation power law, burstiness and Entropy Plots
5. How do they measure the difference in results Simulated the model 10 times for 100,000 nodes, with phost = 0.5 and plink = 0.3. For each run the model exhibited power law in- and out-degree.
6. Are there theoretical results--write the theorems Theorem 1. For a given host, the number of visits an arriving node forms follows power-law out-degree with exponent −2. Proof. Taking phost constant, the expected number of steps y that an arriving node will take is (1/(1−pstep))-1.
7. What are the main conclusions--find it in the abstract and the conclusion Proposal:
-Observe that the non-giant connected components seem to stabilize in size using effective diameter, Burstiness and Entropy Plots.
-Observe the weights on the edges follow several power laws with surprising exponents -- WPL, SPL
-Propose an intuitive, generative model for graph growth that obeys observed patterns – butterflymodel
Conclusion:
Model in robust, as parameters may be chosen over a large range of values and produce realistic graphs.

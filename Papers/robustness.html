Summary

1.Title of the paper The Robustness of Assortativity
2. Noise Model - How did they perturb the network (did they provide an algorithm). Reshuﬄing links while keeping the degree of each node constant. Monte Carlo procedure
3. What data sets did they use (also the number of edges and vertices) Network conﬁgurations obtained by the Barabasi Albert preferential attachment procedure
4. What property/measures of network are they looking at The degree of nodes, eigenvalues of adjacency matrix A of graph (V, E), robustness of network, assortativity coeﬃcient
5. How do they measure the difference in results Laplacian matrix of a network is deﬁned as L ≡ D – A (D is the diagonal matrix). It is the analogous of the Laplacian operator and describes the diﬀusion of random walkers on the network. The eigenvalues of L are λ1 =0≤λ2 ≤ ...≤λN; the eigenvector (mode) associated to the zero-th eigenvalue λ1 is the equilibrium distribution for a diﬀusive process on the network.

To investigate the consequences of the assortativity on the characteristics of a network.
Conclusion:
Disassortative networks have a higher failure threshold, assortative networks give more time for intervention before total breakdown.

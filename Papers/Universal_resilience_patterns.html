Summary

1.Title of the paper Universal resilience patterns in complex networks
2. Noise Model - How did they perturb the network (did they provide an algorithm). Randomly removed a fraction fn of nodes, capturing plant extinctions; Removed a random fraction fl of pollinators, thus perturbing the mutualistic link weights; Finally, randomly rescaled all the weights Aij, reducing their strength on average by a fraction fw to mimic global environmental changes.
3. What data sets did they use (also the number of edges and vertices) Fourteen experimentally mapped networks, plant–pollinator relationships -- collected for seven ecological systems describing networks ranging from N = 10 to N = 1,429 nodes
4. What property/measures of network are they looking at The proposed analytical framework allows us systematically to separate the roles of the system’s dynamics and topology, collapsing the behavior of different networks onto a single universal resilience function
5. How do they measure the difference in results The analytical results unveil the network characteristics that can enhance or diminish resilience, offering ways to prevent the collapse of ecological, biological or economic systems, and guiding the design of technological systems resilient to both internal failures and environmental changes.
6. Are there theoretical results--write the theorems The traditional mathematical treatment of resilience used from ecology to engineering approximates the behavior of a complex system with a one-dimensional (1D) nonlinear dynamic equation Explored the resilience of gene regulatory networks governed by the Michaelis–Menten equation.
7. What are the main conclusions--find it in the abstract and the conclusion Proposal:
The current analytical framework of resilience is designed to treat low-dimensional models with a few interacting components.
Conclusion:
The separation of structure and dynamics provides with testable predictions for the system’s response to different perturbations. It also suggests potential intervention strategies to avoid the loss of resilience, or design principles for optimal resilient systems that can successfully cope with perturbations.
